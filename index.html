I walked into a party one day, and the music sucked.

Winner: BDW PitchFest 2013

piq is the next generation of jukeboxes - a hardware device that went from inception to realization in just three months.

You rarely never have control of music in a social setting. At the very best, it's a benevolent dictatorship. At BDW's Startup class, we decided to break free of this sonic tyranny and herald the arrival of a music democracy.

And that’s why we built piq: a jukebox for social spaces - an experience that allowed empowered you to control what’s playing: at home/on-the-road/ at work/in a coffee shop/in a bar.

It was time to break free of this sonic tyranny: it was time for a music democracy.

My Role

I designed & developed the original piq prototype in a sixteen-hour sprint that involved Caffeine, more Caffeine, jQuery Mobile & a Raspberry PI.

Once we decided to make a business out of it, I took on the role of CEO and focused on the Business Development, Visual Design and Marketing of piq. Of course, as is the case in any startup, you have to do everything. I've talked about my experiences running piq in a post on Medium.

The Pitch - What is piq? How did you build it?

piq is a jukebox for social spaces: a democratic way for anyone to have a say in what plays in the space around you. The tiny $99 hardware device was the first articulation of this idea - connect it to your speakers and a music source of your choice, and you instantly have a crowd-powered jukebox (controlled by web apps for a phone/tablet/PC): one that connects social spaces tightly.

We operated piq on three key principles:

  1. Don’t build anything unless you have to.
  2. When in doubt, ask the customer. When not in doubt, ask anyway.
  3. Run lean, run agile but more than anything else, run.

Creating hardware is tough - especially in a three month timeframe, and this helped us value every decision and task undertaken. This quickly defined piq’s strategy, as we created the barest of prototypes and put them in front of potential customers, allowing them to imagine the product in their context.

From this, we observed patterns that allowed us to build faster, better, and in much less time. It also gave us a following, as 3 sales, 11 installed units, 20 live demos and 500 users guided piq to its launch state.

Branding & Design

piq is an articulation of the essential human yearnings of empowerment (control of music), community (a shared musical experience) and delight (a bloody good song). piq's brand reflected the same.

The name is obviously derived from "pick", the essential act of empowerment, and queues - the familiar context of a jukebox. Paired together, it made a brand that looked suspiciously like two people sharing an experience… which was what we were all about.

The name also provided a memorable, disruptive industrial design which clearly differentiated from an army of Apple TV clones. After multiple prototypes sketched, rendered and 3D Printed, the final form factor was designed to be built with a plastic-injection moulded treatment, it instantly reminded people of a music device and served as a powerful indicator that you can choose what plays here.

The color ways also informed the visual design, allowing for a consistent, unique color palette that emphasized simplicity and high information entropy.

Represent
