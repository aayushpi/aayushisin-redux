This Guy

Hi. I’m Aayush (ah-you-shh) Iyer. I do what’s needed to solve problems using design, code, words & thinking. With over 8 years of experience with design: across advertising, journalism, photography and digital products, I aim to bring empathy, critical thinking and cross-cultural insight to every situation.

I’m an avid fan of Jazz and cinema. I sport a nearly-unhealthy interest in Martial Arts, and care deeply about communities and tribes that are neglected or relegated: especially sex workers and the spectrum of people around them.

This is the last résumé I designed. It’s due for some updates: (till then, here’s a standard résumé

Before moving to Boulder, Colorado to attend the BDW program, I served as the Associate Creative Director at GREY’s Digital arm in Mumbai, spearheading the efforts of brands like Volkswagen and DELL, as well as new business pitches.

In Boulder, I happily made the long-planned jump from advertising and communication to product, by launching piq, a jukebox for social spaces. While the Kickstarter failed spectacularly, I’ve learned much from it. And, who knows, it might will come back in a different form.

With a solid foundation in code and working experience with print, I value tools lesser than the processes used to solve problems. I might design with Sketch on one day, and spend another with XCode - whatever helps solve faster, and better.

At some point in the future, I’d love to have the time to make a personal style guide. For now, here it is: #F03838,#FCE2E0, #2C3E50, ATRAMENT WEB, and Meta Serif Web Pro.

Let’s talk

I’m on a mission with a talented and empathetic team to make meaningful first connections between people at Intro. It’s early days yet, and I can’t wait to share more.

Passion projects in certain disciplines matter immensely for me: if you’d like help with your social justice project (particularly if it focuses on the work of sex work and/or harm reduction for other marginalized communities), send me an email. I’ll get back to you fairly quickly.

Of course, I’m also available to talk about anything interesting that you’re cooking up, or for a cup of coffee. I am pretty easy to reach via Twitter.

This Site

This site was designed and developed within a day, about a year ago. My coding skills were lesser then, but in spite of multiple reviews, I’m quite happy with how it represents me.

If you must know, it’s mostly flat files, connected with a Backbone.js router for modular updates, and optimized at save with an off-the-shelf Grunt script. Of course, there are a few improvements in the pipeline for this site. Namely:

  1. Converting HTML partials to Markdown for easy edits
  2. A little more diversity in layout options for images
  3. PageSpeed optimizations - #1 being inline CSS for the first load
  4. HTTP Request optimizations
  5. Redesigning the mobile template - which in itself is a question about what it means to have a mobile site today

I’ll get to these someday. I like putting stuff away: so here’s the source for this website: you’re free to use any of the code in any way, shape or form. Good pull requests are most appreciated.
