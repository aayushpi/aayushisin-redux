This Guy

Hi. I’m Aayush (ah-you-shh) Iyer.

For work, I do what’s needed to solve problems using design, code, words & thinking. With over 8 years of experience with design: across advertising, journalism, photography and digital products, I aim to bring empathy, critical thinking and cross-cultural insight to every situation.

↓ Resume

More importantly, I’m constantly trying to improve my vernaculars, and recognize my frame of reference / privilege, mitigate its effects and be a little better everyday. I care about intersectionality, and being an ally to marginalized voices. I’m an avid fan of Jazz and cinema, sport a nearly-obsessive interest in Martial Arts.

In India, I’ve worked in many different roles: I wrote about videogames, spent time in Product Management and eventually ended up in advertising. At the end, I served as the Associate Creative Director at GREY’s Digital arm in Mumbai, spearheading the efforts of brands like Volkswagen and DELL, as well as new business pitches.

After moving, I made the long-planned jump from advertising and communication to product, by launching piq, a jukebox for social spaces. While the Kickstarter failed spectacularly, I’ve learned much from it.

Since then, I’ve served as the product designer for a number of startups. I talk about my work (a lot).

Having had experience working with different vernaculars (code, film, print), I value tools lesser than the processes used to solve problems. I might design with Sketch on one day, and spend another with XCode - whatever helps solve faster, and better.

At some point in the future, I’d love to have the time to make a personal style guide. For now, here it is: #F03838,#FCE2E0, #2C3E50, ATRAMENT WEB, and Meta Serif Web Pro.

Let’s talk

I’m looking for my next role: hit me up!

Passion projects in certain disciplines matter immensely for me: if you’re looking for design or product thinking and work with marginalized communities (QTs, POCs, Sex Workers to the front of the queue), I’d love to chat. Similarly, I’m always looking to give back, and actively volunteer and help with issues around education, access, mental health and labor rights.

For all of this, I’m easiest to reach by email or Twitter, and love chatting in person.

This Site

This site was designed and developed within a day, about a year ago. My coding skills were lesser then, but in spite of multiple reviews, I’m quite happy with how it represents me.

If you must know, it’s mostly flat files, connected with a Backbone.js router for modular updates, and optimized at save with an off-the-shelf Grunt script. Of course, there are a few improvements in the pipeline for this site. Namely:

  1. Converting HTML partials to Markdown for easy edits
  2. A little more diversity in layout options for images
  3. PageSpeed optimizations - #1 being inline CSS for the first load
  4. HTTP Request optimizations

I’ll get to these someday. I like putting stuff away: so here’s the source for this website: you’re free to use any of the code in any way, shape or form. Good pull requests are most appreciated.
