Designing for growth

A financial safety-net held by those you trust

Vouch’s mission began with trying to understand what value and identity really mean, from a financial perspective. When we say “You’re more than just your credit score”, it implies the value you’ve built through relationships nurtured at home, at work and everywhere else. By factoring these in our lending philosophy, Vouch allows a whole new, previously underserved, community with an opportunity to access low-cost credit.

Vouch’s first articulation of this vision is a personal loan experience where your application is augmented with the help of sponsors: people you trust that back your credit worthiness by vouching for you: socially, or monetarily. By adding a sponsor, everyone wins: borrowers gain access to larger loan amounts, lower credit & longer terms; Vouch gets additional safeguards against delinquency and fraud; and sponsors… well, they feel great about articulating their trust (and, there’s something to be said about paying it forward too: over a third of all sponsors on Vouch come back and apply for a loan themselves).

And while this is all well and good, change is always difficult: especially in something as entrenched as the lending experience. There’s a huge risk of people feeling uncomfortable with including others in their financial matters, or even adding extra steps in an established user funnel. This makes design at Vouch pretty exciting: in addition to metrics and opportunities in the FinTech space, we also consider many social and consumer opportunities.

Designing for Growth

Vouch invited me to their product design team, tasked with spearheading their growth efforts. This was exciting in a number of ways — it provided great agency to go up and down the funnel as opportunities presented themselves, and allowed me to use, hone and sharpen my faculties in design, development and data-science. Working on growth also allowed for a unique design perspective: focused on metrics.

Implementation

A numeric system looks great - but probably isn't as effective while you're in the shower. Which is why we turned to music. To implement and serve as a catalyst towards responsible water use, we then harnessed a shower's quintessential companion - the song.

An app that’s powered by Spotify (or any popular music service) served an essential compilation of shower AND water friendly songs, which would incentivize participants to enjoy the luxury of a good crooning that's still under the average shower limit.

And if that wasn’t enough, we encouraged the user with an on-demand infographic that encapsulated their savings, music and thoughts: a state-of-the-shower, if you will

My Role

I was responsible for the concept and visual definition of the idea (including its translation to an app and infographic).
